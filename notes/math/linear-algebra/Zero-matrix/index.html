Zero matrix

Last updated Dec 28, 2022 view history

  • Linear algebra

Any matrix that’s full of only zeros, regardless of the dimensions of the matrix, we call a zero matrix. We always name the zero matrix with a capital $O$.

Multiplying a matrix by scalar $0$ results in a zero matrix. $$ 0 \cdot \begin{bmatrix} a & b & c \\ d & f & e \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} $$
